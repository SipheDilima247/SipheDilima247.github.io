What is a Training Academy

One of the strategic foci of ECSA is to establish and monitor initiatives within the ambit of its statutory mandate to promote the professionalisation of the Engineering industry, a profession that is pivotal to the rebuilding, preservation, and ultimately the sustainability of all infrastructure, systems, and technologies that our country needs to improve and sustain quality of life for its citizens.

Flowing from its mandate, ECSA aims to take active steps to reduce the number of engineering graduates being lost in the engineering skills pipeline, and to promote the conversion rate of Candidates to professional status in all of the Council-approved Categories of registration. One of ECSA’s key strategic initiatives that contributes hugely to this cause, is the establishment and certification of public and private Training Academies. Certification is followed by the accreditation of the candidacy mentoring and support programmes of these Academies to assure the quality, scope and depth of such programmes that will prepare Candidates sufficiently for professional application readiness.


A Training Academy is therefore a public or private entity that ECSA awards a formal Certification to, and the accreditation their Candidacy Training and Mentoring programmes.

Who is eligible to be trained at a Training Academy

  • All Candidates that have an accredited, recognized or assessed equivalent qualification and have registered with ECSA as a Candidate.
  • Graduates that require Work Integrated Learning (WIL) to complete their relevant qualification.
  • Experienced “Engineers” who are not professionally registered
